Frequently Asked Questions

Why did you build this?

I built Moogle to scratch a very specific itch.

Email newsletters are hard to find. Your content often sits in someone's inbox unread while people who need it don't even know you exist.

So, I wanted to make a tool to us discover email newsletters. Hence, Moogle. In fact, a later question talks about the name which will make the connection even more obvious.

Who should use Moogle?

At its core, Moogle converts an email into a webpage. Once this happens, that webpage can be used in so many ways!

A customer support team could use Moogle to jointly review and discuss a customer email before actioning it.

Marketing teams team could use Moogle to jointly review and discuss an outbound email before dispatch.

You could use Moogle to archive old emails as web pages. Or download it as a simple .html file.

Teachers could use Moogle to gather feedback from students.

Companies could use Moogle to share emailed testimonials from customers.

Newsletters could use Moogle to share their emails with non-subscribers.

You could even use Moogle to design web pages on a mail client like Mailchimp® or mjml.io®, then convert them to html and host the html yourself!

You can bookmark the link in Pocket® for later reading, share it on WhatsApp® or Slack®, monitor its long term search performance, or build another channel for engagement people who did not receive your email.

How to Moogle
  1. Include bookmarks@moogle.cc on any email*
  2. We'll Reply-All with a shortlink
  3. Now, anyone can visit that shortlink to bookmark, download, or discuss that email
  4. Done!

*You can forward old emails, cc us on existing threads, or simply email us to start Moogling
